Spatial Frequencies

Our percetion of edges and detail in an image comes down to spatial freequency. Spatial frequency refers to the amount of detail a given image or object has per cycle where in a black white image 1 cycle would be a unit of light followed by dark or vice versa. This can be visualised in the sinusoidal graph below. Images that have low spatial frequency tend to have smaller difference in change where as images with high spatial frequency change drastically.
